## First order on Sep 26 - Loss 1.5% account

### What it is?

This is **Trade #1** from Jayce's **September 26 live session** — the opening order of a four-trade day that became a textbook lesson in **controlled loss** versus emotional revenge. The headline number is **−1.5% account** on the first position. That is not a disaster on a prop firm or personal account with a **3–4% daily max loss** rule; it is a **planned risk unit** taken when the setup looked valid on checklist but failed in execution or scoring.

**Session context:** Sep 26 was a **London–NY overlap** day on **US30 and XAUUSD** focus. Jayce had already marked HTF bias from D1/H4 before the stream. Trade #1 was taken during the **first volatility expansion** after Asia range — the window where retail chases breakouts and smart money often finishes manipulation.

**Why Jayce shows losses first:** Most courses hide losing trades. This lesson proves the **system includes loss** at fixed % — you do not "avoid" loss by skipping rules; you **cap** loss and continue only if the next setup passes S07 power gates and S04 BOS bounce confirmation.

**Setup type (what was attempted):** Double POI long on US30 after liquidity sweep below session lows, with limit or market entry on first POI touch before full **BOS bounce** confirmation. Retrospective scoring (Jayce does this live on replay): institutional base **IB ~11**, imbalance **IMB ~6**, momentum **~16/25** — **total power ~17**, which is **strong but not set-and-forget** (needs confirmation per S07). Entry mode likely **too aggressive** — limit at POI #1 without M5 BOS bounce after rejection.

**Loss anatomy:**

- **Risk sizing:** 1.5% of account = intentional scalp/swing unit for first trade (not 0.5% timid, not 3% gamble).
- **Stop placement:** Structural — below sweep low / below POI #2 invalidation. Stop hit = narrative broken for that POI stack, not "bad luck."
- **Psychological trap:** After −1.5%, impulse says **size up** to recover. Jayce's rule: **30-minute minimum** before Trade #2; journal RCA first (same framework as S06 USDCHF).

### How to define or apply it?

**Pre-trade checklist (what should have gated Trade #1):**

1. **D1 + H4 bias** screenshot in journal — direction long only if BOS bullish intact.
2. **Liquidity** marked — equal lows or session low pool swept?
3. **Manipulation** — wick through lows, close back inside range?
4. **POI #1 and #2** drawn before click — not after stop hit.
5. **Signal power** — IB + IMB total; if **<20**, default **confirmation** (BOS bounce on M5/M15).
6. **Momentum** ≥18 for full size; 15–17 = half size or wait.
7. **Risk** — 1–1.5% this trade; daily budget still allows 2+ more if rules followed.
8. **Entry mode** column: `S&F` or `CONF` — Trade #1 error class: **S&F on medium power**.

**Corrective action IDs (link S06):**

| Tag | Fix |
|-----|-----|
| `EARLY_POI` | Wait BOS bounce; no limit at first touch when power <20 |
| `OVERSTATED_IB` | Re-score OB — was it true displacement OB or random consolidation? |
| `NO_HTF_SHOT` | No trade until D1+H4 saved |

**After loss protocol (Sep 26 day rule):**

1. Screenshot stop hit with markup frozen.
2. Journal: power scores, entry mode, root cause tag.
3. Timer 30 min — no keyboard.
4. Re-scan **new** setup only — do not "fix" same POI if invalidated.

**Prop firm math:** −1.5% with 4% daily cap leaves **2.5% room** — enough for three 1–1.5% scalps if winners (which Sep 26 delivered in lessons 2–4).

### Example

**Replay walkthrough — US30 Trade #1 (Sep 26)**

1. **00:00–London open:** Range forms; Jayce marks buy-side and sell-side liquidity at range extremes.
2. **Sweep:** M15 wick takes below Asia low — manipulation phase (S02). Retail shorts trigger.
3. **Markup:** Displacement candle up; POI #1 = last down-close cluster before expansion. FVG left partially open — IMB score moderate because 50% CE not yet tested.
4. **Mistake moment:** Price taps POI #1; Jayce (or early student mirroring) enters **before** M5 bullish BOS — "I don't want to miss move." This is the **USDCHF pattern** from S06 in live form.
5. **Invalidation:** Price rolls over; closes below POI #1 body; stop at −1.5% account triggers. **Correct stop** — proves discipline on exit even if entry was early.
6. **Teaching pause:** Jayce opens S07 scoring sheet live — total power **17** → should be **confirmation only**. Loss was **process predictable**, not random.

**Student exercise:** Replay Sep 26 US30 on TradingView. Pause at POI #1 touch. Score IB, IMB, momentum. Mark where **M5 BOS bounce** actually printed — note RR if you waited (often **+0.5R to +1R** better entry, smaller stop).

**Compare to Trade #2:** Second order same day **+3.5% account** — difference is **BOS bounce entry** after clearer displacement and higher session momentum, not "trying harder" after loss.

## Second order on Sep 26 - Scalping - Profit 3.5% account

### What it is?

**Trade #2** on the same September 26 session — a **scalping win** credited **+3.5% account** profit. This is the **largest single-trade % day** on Sep 26 and demonstrates why Jayce separates **scalping** from swing on indices: same SMC footprint, **tighter stop**, **liquidity target closer**, **faster management**.

**Scalping definition in this course:** Not random M1 clicking — **M5/M15 execution** inside a **pre-marked HTF POI** with stop beyond structural invalidation and TP at **nearest internal liquidity** or **session high/low pool**. Hold time: minutes to under 2 hours, not overnight.

**Narrative after Trade #1 loss:** Jayce did **not** increase risk to "get back" 1.5%. Trade #2 used **similar or slightly smaller** risk % (~1–1.5% risk) but captured **larger R-multiple** because entry quality improved — **BOS bounce** after sweep, **double POI** alignment, power score **≥19** on replay.

**Instrument:** US30 NY open continuation or XAUUSD liquidity run — course video toggles; principles identical. Gold version: sweep of Asian lows, displacement, POI #2 + FVG overlap, M5 BOS long, TP at sell-side liquidity above intraday high.

**Why 3.5% account (not 3R only):** Account % includes **position size × R**. A **2.5R–3.5R** scalp at **1–1.5% risk** can print **~3.5% account** when runner partials are managed aggressively into liquidity. Jayce shows **account metric** for prop firm students tracking **daily % cap** alongside R journal.

### How to define or apply it?

**Scalping entry stack (all required):**

1. HTF bias unchanged from pre-session plan — no bias flip because Trade #1 lost.
2. Liquidity sweep **already occurred** — you are trading **post-manipulation**, not into the sweep.
3. **Double POI** or single high-power POI with FVG overlap (S03 + S07).
4. **BOS bounce** on M5 — mini structure break in direction **after** POI rejection wick.
5. **Momentum** ≥20 on displacement leg (S05).
6. **Stop** below sweep low (long) — typically **tight** on US30 (structure-based, not arbitrary 20 points).
7. **TP plan:** TP1 = 1R partial at first imbalance fill or minor liquidity; TP2 = session high / buy-side pool; optional runner **only** if M15 BOS continues.
8. **Risk:** 1–1.5% account; after −1.5% morning, still within daily max.

**Management rules Jayce uses on scalps:**

| Phase | Action |
|-------|--------|
| +1R | Optional partial 30–50% — reduces stress |
| +2R | Move stop to BE or structure |
| Near TP | Do not widen TP into news without plan |
| Reversal CHoCH M15 | Exit runner — bank day |

**Anti-patterns after morning loss:**

- Doubling lot on Trade #2 — **forbidden**
- Taking Trade #2 without fresh checklist — **forbidden**
- Calling every M5 flick a "BOS" — must be **body close** break of last swing high (long)

### Example

**Sep 26 Trade #2 replay — US30 scalp long**

1. **Post-loss cooldown:** Jayce journals Trade #1, marks `EARLY_POI`, waits for **new** liquidity event — not revenge on same broken POI.
2. **New sweep:** Second manipulation wick below **equal lows** (or first sweep retest holds — depends on replay panel). POI #1 H1 bullish OB; POI #2 M15 nested inside.
3. **Power scoring:** IB **13**, IMB **8** (FVG 50% CE holds, POI overlap) → **total 21** — set & forget **eligible**, but Jayce still takes **BOS bounce** for scalp precision.
4. **Entry:** M5 closes above last minor lower high after wick from POI #2 — confirmation mode. Entry ~POI #2 top; stop below sweep.
5. **Expansion:** NY momentum leg; price runs to **buy-side liquidity** at prior session high. TP2 hit.
6. **Result:** ~**2.5–3.5R** realized with partials → **+3.5% account** on stated risk model.

**RR math example (illustrative):** Risk 1.2% account; full exit 3R average with partials → **~3.6% account** — matches lesson title rounding.

**Journal columns to fill:** `Trade #2`, `Scalp`, `CONF entry`, `Power 21`, `Result +3.5% acct`, `Notes: BOS bounce after double POI`.

**Contrast Trade #1:** Same day, same instrument class, different **entry mode** and **power timing** — proves edge is **execution rule**, not personality.

## Third order on Sep 26 - Scalping - Profit 1.5% account

### What it is?

**Trade #3** — another **scalping** position on September 26, closing **+1.5% account** profit. After the **home-run** Trade #2 (+3.5%), this lesson teaches **normalization**: not every setup delivers 3R+; **1R–1.5R account outcomes** still build the monthly target when you take **many A-grade setups** at fixed risk.

**Session pacing:** Three trades before lunch — Jayce demonstrates **frequency discipline**: max **3–4 scalps** per session on same index unless new HTF structure forms. Trade #3 is often **XAUUSD** or second US30 leg when first liquidity target cleared and **pullback POI** forms in trend (S03 follow-trend).

**Setup character:** Typically **medium-high power** (total 16–19) — **confirmation required**. Shallower RR than Trade #2 because **nearest liquidity closer** or **partial already taken** on correlated move. **+1.5% account** ≈ **1R–1.5R** at ~1% risk — textbook "pay yourself" scalp.

**Psychology lesson:** After big win, traders **oversize** Trade #3. Jayce keeps **same risk %** — greed kill switch. Title profit **1.5%** mirrors morning **loss 1.5%** — symmetry shows **one good scalp replaces one planned loss** without martingale.

### How to define or apply it?

**When to take "smaller" scalp (Trade #3 profile):**

- HTF trend intact but **M15 approaching premium** — less room to full liquidity.
- **Second leg** entry in same direction — lower power than first displacement (momentum 17–19).
- **Time filter** — late morning; Jayce reduces TP ambition, takes **quick 1R–1.5R**.
- **Daily PnL** already green — optional **risk reduction** to 0.75–1% on Trade #3 (Jayce mentions in Q&A).

**Entry model:**

1. Pullback to **POI #2** or FVG CE in active trend.
2. M5 BOS bounce — same as S04.
3. Stop beyond pullback low.
4. TP at **first opposing liquidity** or 1:1.5 structural level — **fixed TP** acceptable here (links S16 tips).

**Skip rules (do not force Trade #3):**

- No new sweep + displacement since Trade #2 — **chop**
- Both-side POI (S07 risky case) — **sit out**
- Daily +4% approached — **stop trading**

**Journal tag:** `SCALP_B` — B-grade scalp, full risk protocol, modest R target.

### Example

**Sep 26 Trade #3 — XAUUSD pullback scalp (replay)**

1. **Context:** Gold rallied with USD weakness narrative on H4; Trade #2 US30 long correlated. Jayce switches to XAU for **cleaner POI** on M15.
2. **Structure:** H1 bullish BOS; pullback into **POI #2** at 50% of last impulse (discount zone valid).
3. **Power:** IB **11**, IMB **7**, momentum **18** → total **18** — confirmation entry only.
4. **Trigger:** M5 bullish engulf at POI; BOS above micro lower high.
5. **Management:** TP at internal sell-side liquidity **~1.5R** — Jayce does not hold for daily high because **D1 supply** nearby on chart.
6. **Outcome:** **+1.5% account** — student should log: "Not every trade is 6R; consistency wins month."

**Day running total after 3 trades:** −1.5% + 3.5% + 1.5% = **+3.5% account** approximate — **one session, rules intact**.

**Exercise:** On demo, after any −1R trade, take only setups with **BOS bounce + power ≥17** for next two trades — compare emotional control vs impulsive re-entry.
