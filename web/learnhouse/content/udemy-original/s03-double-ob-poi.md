# S03: Main — Double Order-Block with POI - POI to optimize RR

## Trading follow trend_Theory

### What it is?

**Trading follow trend** means aligning with smart money's dominant direction — you trade **pullbacks to POI** after BOS, not random counter-trend guesses. In an uptrend: liquidity swept below, bullish BOS, pullback to **discount** double order-block, long with stop below OB, target BSL above. In a downtrend: mirror — premium POI, short, target SSL. This is the **highest win-rate** mode in the SMC system because you ride institutional flow instead of fighting it. Jayce built the entire prop firm challenge curriculum around follow-trend because funded accounts die from drawdown, not from missed reversals. One follow-trend month at 65% win rate and 1:4 average RR compounds; one month of counter-trend guessing at 40% win rate bleeds the account regardless of occasional big wins.

**Double order-block with POI** is the engine: first OB on HTF (H4/D1) defines the **zone of interest**; second OB on LTF (H1/M15) nested inside refines **entry** so stop is tight and RR is optimized (often 1:3 to 1:8 on XAUUSD swings). POI is not "any OB" — it is the intersection of double OB, correct premium/discount, post-liquidity context, and BOS confirmation. Jayce built this for **prop firm challenges** where profit % matters less than **low drawdown** and consistent Set & Forget execution. The math is simple: if HTF target is 40 points away and LTF POI lets you stop 5 points instead of 15, your RR triples on the same trade idea.

Follow-trend trades sit in Phase 4–5 of the footprint: liquidity tested, imbalance created, manipulation may occur on pullback, then price returns to POI where **retail and institutions trade together** in the true direction. Signal power peaks when HTF and LTF both show same bias and double OB overlaps FVG. You are not predicting tops or bottoms — you are identifying where smart money already committed direction via BOS, then waiting for price to offer you the same entry they defended. That patience is the entire skill. Markup phase on the market cycle (S04) is where follow-trend prints money; distribution is where you switch to break-trend.

The theory lesson exists so every live example in the next lesson repeats the same checklist until it becomes automatic. Jayce's students who fail prop challenges almost always fail because they enter before BOS confirms, enter at premium in uptrend, or trade single OB without HTF overlap. The theory is the contract: HTF bias → liquidity swept → double OB in correct EQ half → LTF BOS → Set & Forget. Deviate from any step and you are gambling with extra steps. Follow trend is not exciting — it is profitable. Excitement is manipulation phase; profit is POI entry after the footprint completes.

### How to define or apply it?

1. **HTF bias:** D1/H4 trend via BOS sequence — only long in HH/HL, only short in LH/LL.
2. Mark **HTF orderblock** after last displacement leg in trend direction.
3. Drop to H1/M15; wait for **pullback** into HTF OB zone (discount for longs, premium for shorts).
4. Mark **LTF orderblock** inside HTF zone — this is your **double OB POI**.
5. Require **LTF BOS** in trend direction before entry (continuation confirm).
6. Entry: limit or confirmation at LTF POI; stop beyond LTF OB + wick buffer.
7. Target: next HTF liquidity pool (BSL/SSL) or partial at EQ then runner.
8. Draw EQ on current dealing range — verify POI sits in discount (long) or premium (short).
9. Score signal power: trend alignment, sweep, FVG, double OB, BOS — minimum 4/5 for full size.
10. No entry during manipulation spike into POI — wait for rejection candle or BOS bounce.
11. **Chart rule:** No double OB overlap = no trade — single isolated OB is secondary setup.
12. **Chart rule:** CHoCH against HTF on LTF during pullback = skip — trend may be breaking (see break-trend module).
13. **Chart rule:** Pullback that closes through HTF OB with body = OB invalid, re-mark after new BOS.
14. **Chart rule:** Minimum RR 1:2.5 before Set & Forget — skip marginal POIs with tight targets.
15. Optimize RR: tighter LTF POI inside HTF = larger RR to same target; journal every trade's RR at entry.

### Example

**Historical XAUUSD — D1/H4/H1/M15 follow trend long framework, April 2024.**

**HTF bias:** D1 clear uptrend — HH at **2280**, HL at **2250**, HH at **2320**. H4 bullish BOS sequence intact. SSL at **2300** (prior month low) identified as next liquidity target below current price.

**Double OB POI:**
- H4 bullish OB: **2310–2315** (discount — below EQ 2310... note: lower edge at EQ, core in discount)
- H1 nested bullish OB: **2312–2314**
- FVG overlap: **2311–2313**
- POI sweet spot: **2312.50**

**Pullback:** Three-day drift from **2320** into H4 zone. No H4 CHoCH during pullback — character still bullish. Minor H1 manipulation dip to **2311** (absorbing at FVG CE) rejected with pin bar.

**Retail mistake:** Bought **2325** (premium, above EQ) on "momentum" — stopped on pullback to POI at 2312. Same direction, wrong zone, wrong RR. POI at 2313 captured full leg.

## Trading follow trend_Example

### What it is?

This lesson walks a **complete follow-trend case** on chart — same rules as theory but with candle-by-candle narration. You see liquidity sweep, FVG creation, shallow pullback into **double OB POI**, BOS entry trigger, and Set & Forget management to liquidity target. The goal is muscle memory: when you replay 20 such examples, your eye finds POI in seconds during London/NY gold session. Theory tells you the rules; this lesson shows you the **movie** — frame by frame, phase by phase, candle by candle.

Follow-trend example emphasizes **patience** — many students mark OB too early before pullback completes. Jayce waits for price to enter HTF zone, print LTF OB, then BOS. Entry before BOS is anticipation (retail); entry after BOS at POI is **confirmation** (smart money style). Profit % compounds when you skip marginal setups that lack double OB overlap. The example case is deliberately "clean" — not every live day looks this perfect, but you hunt for this profile every session. When 4 of 6 checklist items align, you watch. When 6 of 6 align, you Set & Forget.

The walkthrough uses three timeframes simultaneously: H4 for bias and outer OB, H1 for inner OB and pullback structure, M15 for BOS entry trigger. This is the standard Jayce desk setup for XAUUSD swing trades. Scalpers drop to M5 for trigger inside M15 POI (S04 BOS bounce), but the double OB marking stays on H4/H1. The example also demonstrates what **not** to do: the student who bought the BOS candle at **2312** before pullback got a drawdown scare when price dipped to **2306**; the POI trader at **2306.50** had calm drawdown and full TP. Time and zone — not prediction.

Jayce narrates live sessions the same way this lesson reads: "Liquidity swept. FVG printed. Pullback entering H4 zone. H1 OB forming. Wait. M15 BOS — now we act." That narration becomes your internal monologue. By example 20, you hear it automatically when you open the chart. This lesson is the most replayed in the course because it bridges theory and live execution.

### How to define or apply it?

1. Open **XAUUSD H4** — identify trend and dealing range EQ.
2. Mark BSL/SSL and note last sweep direction.
3. Highlight **HTF OB** from last bearish/bullish candle before impulse.
4. Switch **H1** — watch pullback enter HTF zone; draw LTF OB.
5. Confirm **premium/discount** — long only if POI below EQ of current leg.
6. Drop **M15** — wait for BOS in trend direction; set alert at POI.
7. Place order: entry POI, stop structural, TP1 partial at internal liquidity, TP2 main BSL/SSL.
8. Label phases 0–5 on chart before placing order — if any phase missing, reduce size or skip.
9. Screenshot entry with full footprint labeled — journal entry within 5 minutes of fill.
10. Record RR at entry, not at close — discipline metric for prop firm rules.
11. **Chart rule:** If pullback violates HTF OB with body close, OB invalid — re-mark after new BOS.
12. **Chart rule:** News spike through POI without rejection = wait for new structure, no revenge entry.
13. **Chart rule:** Do not move stop to breakeven until TP1 hit or H4 BOS in trade direction.
14. **Chart rule:** If M15 CHoCH against trade during pullback, abort — trend may be shifting.
15. Screenshot full footprint for journal: phases 0–5 labeled on chart.

## Trading break the trend_Theory

### What it is?

**Trading break the trend** is the reversal playbook — you stop following pullback longs in uptrend when smart money signals **distribution complete**. Triggers: liquidity sweep at trend extreme (BSL in uptrend), **bearish CHoCH** on HTF or strong LTF, manipulation spike into premium, then entry at **bearish double OB POI** targeting SSL and opposite dealing range. Mirror for bullish reversal in downtrend (SSL sweep, bullish CHoCH, discount POI, target BSL). Break-trend is how you capture the **whole markdown leg** after weeks of markup — one Case 3 reversal can equal ten follow-trend scalps in profit %.

Break-trend is **lower frequency, higher impact** than follow-trend. Win rate can be slightly lower but RR often larger (whole swing reversal). Mistakes come from calling every CHoCH a reversal — Jayce requires **liquidity + CHoCH + premium/discount POI + double OB** minimum. Without sweep at top/bottom, CHoCH may be minor correction, not trend break. The amateur sees CHoCH on M15 and shorts; the operator sees CHoCH on M15 inside H4 uptrend without BSL sweep and waits. HTF context governs everything.

This theory connects to market cycle (S04): accumulation → markup → distribution → markdown. Break-trend trades often initiate at **distribution** after retail is maximum long at premium. Social media is bullish, analysts raise targets, retail piles in — and smart money sweeps BSL, prints CHoCH, and distributes into every buy order at premium POI. Signal power rises when follow-trend POIs fail repeatedly (failed BOS, strong CHoCH, breaker blocks). When your third follow-trend long in a week stops out at POI, stop looking for longs — the cycle is shifting.

Jayce teaches break-trend **after** follow-trend because students who learn reversals first become chronic top-pickers. Master riding the trend, then learn when to flip. The theory cases (1, 2, 3) in following lessons show clean and messy reversals. Case 3 is the gold standard; Cases 1 and 2 are what live markets actually deliver 60% of the time. Theory prepares you for all three profiles so one stopped-out POI does not destroy your psychology or your account.

### How to define or apply it?

1. Identify **exhausted trend** — multiple BOS, weak displacement, liquidity resting beyond extreme.
2. Wait for **liquidity sweep** beyond trend high (uptrend) or low (downtrend).
3. Confirm **CHoCH** on H4 or H1 — body close breaking last HL (bearish) or LH (bullish).
4. Mark **HTF reversal OB** at manipulation extreme (premium for shorts, discount for longs).
5. Find **LTF nested OB** inside HTF for tight stop — double OB POI for reversal.
6. Optional: **breaker block** — old bullish OB that failed becomes bearish POI.
7. Entry after LTF CHoCH aligns with HTF reversal bias.
8. Target: EQ of range then opposite liquidity (SSL after top sweep).
9. Verify premium/discount — reversal shorts must be in premium, reversal longs in discount.
10. Reduce size 25–50% vs follow-trend until CHoCH confirmed on HTF.
11. **Chart rule:** One CHoCH without sweep = scalp only, not full break-trend swing.
12. **Chart rule:** HTF still strong BOS trend = break-trend is counter — reduce size or wait HTF CHoCH.
13. **Chart rule:** If H4 BOS re-establishes trend after your CHoCH, exit immediately — reversal failed.
14. **Chart rule:** Break-trend POI gets maximum 2 mitigation attempts (Case 1) — third touch skip.
15. Journal "break-trend" tag separately from "follow-trend" — track win rate and avg RR per mode.

### Example

**Historical EURUSD — D1/H4/H1 break-trend short theory, May 2024.**

**Context:** Multi-week EURUSD uptrend **1.0750→1.0950**. D1 markup mature. Retail overwhelmingly long. BSL built at **1.0945–1.0955** (equal highs, 3 touches). Jayce weekly prep: "Distribution zone approaching — stop follow-trend longs above 1.0920."

**Phase 0 — Test liquidity:** Thursday NY swept BSL to **1.0962**, H4 wicked above **1.0955**, **closed back below 1.0945** — buy-stops triggered, rejection confirmed. Phase 0 complete at premium extreme.

**Phase 2 — Imbalance:** Bearish H4 FVG **1.0935–1.0925** during displacement candle post-sweep.

**Phase 3 — Manipulation:** Friday Asian pushed to **1.0958** (failed retest of sweep high), rejected with H4 shooting star — manipulation into premium complete.

**CHoCH:** H4 **bearish CHoCH** — body close below HL **1.0920** at **1.0915**. Character changed from bullish to bearish on H4. Gate open for break-trend shorts.

**Double OB POI:**
- H4 bearish OB: **1.0935–1.0942** (premium — above EQ 1.0850 of range 1.0750–1.0950)
- H1 nested OB: **1.0938–1.0940**

**Timeline rule:** Follow-trend longs from **1.0900** POI **before** CHoCH were valid (markup). **After** CHoCH at **1.0920**, only break-trend shorts per rules. Longs at **1.0910** post-CHoCH fought smart money — stopped.

## Trading break the trend_Example_case 3

### What it is?

**Case 3** is the **cleanest break-trend** profile — full SMC footprint visible: sweep, manipulation, CHoCH, premium double OB POI, expansion to SSL. Jayce uses Case 3 to teach the **gold standard** reversal before showing messier Cases 1 & 2 where first POI fails or structure is ambiguous. Case 3 is what you hunt on US30 and XAUUSD weekly reversals for large RR Set & Forget. It appears perhaps 2–4 times per month on gold — not daily, but each instance can deliver 1:5 to 1:10 RR on swing holds.

Case 3 typically occurs after **extended trend** + **obvious retail positioning** (everyone talking about "new highs"). Smart money sweeps, prints clear CHoCH on H4, returns once to POI, never gives second chance — displacement aggressive. Students learn: **missed POI = wait next cycle**, do not chase. The signature of Case 3 is the **single POI touch** — price mitigates the double OB once, rejects cleanly, and expands without deep retest. If price returns to POI a second time, you are likely in Case 1 territory and must adjust size and stop protocol.

Case 3 connects every definition from S02: liquidity sweep at trend extreme, FVG during displacement, manipulation into premium, CHoCH on HTF, POI at double OB overlap, expansion to SSL. Signal power 5–6/6. Jayce's funded account "home run" trades are almost all Case 3 profiles on XAUUSD or US30. They are not scalps — they are weekly holds with fixed risk and distant liquidity targets. The emotional challenge is not entry; it is holding through minor M15 pullbacks that tempt you to close early. Set & Forget means Set & Forget.

This lesson exists because students who only study Case 3 believe every reversal is clean. When Case 1 stops them out on first POI, they abandon the system. Jayce shows Case 3 first to establish the **ideal fingerprint**, then Cases 1 & 2 teach adaptation. Know Case 3 by heart; recognize when the market delivers Case 1 or 2 instead; never force Case 3 narrative onto a messy chart.

### How to define or apply it?

1. Label chart phases 0–5 explicitly before entry.
2. Verify **sweep** wick beyond key high/low with rejection close.
3. Mark manipulation leg into **premium** (short) or **discount** (long).
4. CHoCH must be **body close** on H4 or H1 — screenshot for journal.
5. Draw double OB; set limit at LTF POI 50% of OB if momentum strong.
6. Stop above manipulation high + OB wick (shorts) or below (longs).
7. First TP at nearest FVG fill or EQ; runner at major SSL/BSL.
8. Case 3 entry is full size (100% risk unit) — maximum confluence warrants maximum allowed size.
9. No stop to breakeven until TP1 hit — Case 3 expansions can wick 30% back before continuing.
10. **Chart rule:** Case 3 = one clean POI touch — if deep mitigation twice, downgrade to Case 2 rules.
11. Compare **signal power score** (liquidity, CHoCH, premium, double OB, FVG) — Case 3 needs 5+.
12. **Chart rule:** If no POI touch within 5 sessions of CHoCH, setup expired — wait new cycle.
13. **Chart rule:** Case 3 short requires premium POI — if POI in discount after CHoCH, it is Case 2 deep profile.
14. **Chart rule:** Do not add to Case 3 position on retest — Case 3 does not retest; adding means you missed Case 1/2.
15. Backtest 10 Case 3 reversals on XAUUSD 2023–2024; log win rate and avg RR.

## Trading break the trend_Example_case 1 & 2

### What it is?

**Case 1** and **Case 2** are **real-world messier** break-trend reversals — not every top looks like Case 3. **Case 1:** CHoCH prints early, first POI mitigation gets **stopped** (stop hunt above OB), then **second POI** (breaker block or deeper HTF OB) holds — two-chance entry. **Case 2:** CHoCH valid but pullback is **deep** — price mitigates HTF OB fully, tests 62–79% premium/discount, LTF double OB at range extreme; requires wider stop but larger TP. Together with Case 3, you have the complete reversal matrix for live market.

Jayce pairs Cases 1 & 2 in one lesson because traders quit after Case 1 stop-out thinking "reversal failed" — smart money often **tests POI twice** before markdown. The first POI stop hunt is not system failure; it is institutions clearing remaining stops above the OB before the real move. Understanding Case 1 prevents revenge trading and teaches the **breaker block** entry — one of the highest RR setups in the entire course. Case 2 teaches patience for deep retracements that shallow POI traders miss entirely.

Case 1 win rate per **individual entry** is lower (~45–55%) but per **setup** (counting both attempts) rises to ~65–70%. Case 2 win rate is moderate (~55–60%) with larger average RR (1:4 to 1:6) because the deep pullback offers entry closer to the range extreme with target at the opposite extreme. Jayce's rule: identify which case **before** placing the order — this adjusts size (Case 1 first entry = half size), stop width (Case 2 = wider), and expectations (Case 3 = no second chance).

The emotional discipline for Cases 1 & 2 is harder than Case 3. Case 3 feels clean and confident. Case 1 hurts when the first stop hits. Case 2 bores you waiting 3–5 days for price to reach deep premium. Both require trusting the CHoCH gate — if CHoCH remains valid (no BOS re-establishing trend), the narrative is alive even when first entry fails. Abort only when structure aborts, not when emotions abort.

### How to define or apply it?

**Case 1 — double mitigation:**
1. Full break-trend checklist same as Case 3 (sweep, CHoCH, premium/discount).
2. First entry at LTF POI — use **half size**; expect stop hunt above/below OB.
3. Stop beyond manipulation wick — accept -1R on first attempt as normal Case 1 cost.
4. Re-mark **breaker block** (failed OB that flips) or second nested OB after stop hunt.
5. CHoCH still valid if no BOS re-establishing trend on H4.
6. Second entry at new POI; stop beyond new manipulation extreme; **full size** if signal power restored.
7. Net setup target: +1R to +3R combined across both attempts.

**Case 2 — deep pullback:**
1. CHoCH + sweep confirmed; shallow POI fails to attract price or fills without rejection.
2. Price retraces **deep** into 62–79% premium (shorts) or discount (longs).
3. Use **HTF OB as primary** POI; LTF for timing only.
4. Wider stop below/above HTF OB (10–20 pips forex, $8–15 gold).
5. Target full range to opposite liquidity — RR still >1:3 despite wider stop.
6. Wait 2–5 H4 candles — patience is the entry trigger.

**Shared rules:**
7. Abort if H4 BOS **re-establishes** trend direction after CHoCH.
8. Journal Case number (1, 2, or 3) on every break-trend trade for stats.
9. Case 1 first entry never full size — protocol not optional.
10. **Chart rule:** Case 2 needs patience — 2–5 days on H4 common on gold.
11. **Chart rule:** If second Case 1 POI also fails, trend reversal narrative dead — stand aside.
12. **Chart rule:** Deep Case 2 POI below 79% retrace may signal full trend resumption — verify CHoCH.
13. Compare all three cases on same instrument monthly — which case appears most often?
14. **Chart rule:** Breaker block POI must form after CHoCH — not before.
15. Track net R per setup (not per entry) for Case 1 — proves two-shot protocol works.

### Example

**Case 1 — Historical GBPUSD, H4/H1, July 2024.**

**Context:** Uptrend into **1.2850**, BSL at **1.2845–1.2855**. Sweep to **1.2862**, H4 CHoCH bearish below **1.2820** (body close **1.2818**). Distribution phase.

**First POI attempt:** H1 bearish OB **1.2840–1.2845** in premium. Entry short **1.2842**, stop **1.2865**. Price wicked to **1.2868** — stop hunt above OB, stopped **-1R**. CHoCH still valid (no H4 close above 1.2820).

**Breaker block:** Failed bullish OB at **1.2848** (the candle that wicked stops) flipped to bearish POI on retest. Second POI short **1.2846**, stop **1.2872**, target **1.2780** SSL.
