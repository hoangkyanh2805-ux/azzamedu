## Simple Wyckoff and Elliot

### What it is?

Wyckoff and Elliott Wave are **two classic frameworks** that describe how price moves through accumulation, markup, distribution, and markdown — and how those moves often unfold in repeating wave patterns. In this course we do **not** teach full Wyckoff certification or Elliott Wave degree counting. We teach the **simple version** that connects directly to Smart Money Concepts you already learned: liquidity, manipulation, institutional base, BOS/CHoCH, and POI.

**Wyckoff (simple SMC lens):**

- **Phase A–E** on a range: smart money tests liquidity below/above the range, absorbs retail orders, then marks up or down.
- **Spring / Upthrust** = manipulation phase you already mark as liquidity sweep before POI entry.
- **Sign of Strength (SOS) / Sign of Weakness (SOW)** = displacement candles leaving imbalance — same footprint as FVG after manipulation.
- **Creek / ICE** = range boundaries — map to key level and premium/discount zones.

**Elliott (simple SMC lens):**

- Impulse waves **1–2–3–4–5** in trend direction; corrective waves **A–B–C** against trend.
- Wave 2 and Wave 4 often pull back into **order block / POI** where institutions add positions.
- Wave 3 is usually the **strongest momentum leg** — where double POI + BOS continuation pays best RR.
- You do not need to argue "this is wave 4.382" on Twitter. You need: *Is price in impulse or correction on HTF?* That answer filters which SMC setup you trade.

Together, Wyckoff tells you **where smart money is building position**; Elliott tells you **which leg of the move you are in**. SMC gives you the **entry map** (POI, imbalance, BOS bounce).

### How to define or apply it?

**Step 1 — Mark HTF range or trend (Wyckoff context)**

On D1 or H4 for XAUUSD / US30:

1. Identify last clear **accumulation range** (sideways after decline) or **distribution range** (sideways after rally).
2. Label manipulation: did price sweep range low (spring) or range high (upthrust)?
3. After sweep + displacement, mark **institutional base** inside the range — that becomes your POI zone for continuation.

**Step 2 — Count impulse vs correction (Elliott context — minimum)**

On H1 or M15 execution TF:

1. After BOS in trend direction, assume you are in **impulse** until CHoCH against your bias.
2. First pullback after BOS = often **wave 2** or **wave 4** style correction — look for POI + imbalance confluence.
3. If correction is deep, overlapping, and no clear BOS continuation — treat as **ABC correction**; reduce size or wait for new range break.

**Step 3 — Merge with SMC checklist**

| Wyckoff/Elliott read | SMC action |
|----------------------|------------|
| Post-spring, SOS up | Buy POI in discount after liquidity sweep |
| Post-upthrust, SOW down | Sell POI in premium after liquidity sweep |
| Impulse wave 3 context | Trade trend-follow double POI with BOS bounce |
| Deep ABC correction | No trade or break-trend setup only with extra confirmation |
| Distribution complete | Look for sell POI on retest of breakdown OB |

**Rule Jayce uses:** If Wyckoff phase and Elliott wave read **conflict** with your SMC POI (e.g., you want long but HTF is clear distribution), **skip**. Extra knowledge is a **filter**, not an excuse to force trades.

### Example

**XAUUSD H4 — accumulation spring into wave 3 style markup**

1. Gold compresses into a range after a decline. Equal lows sit below the range — retail stops cluster there.
2. Price sweeps below range low (Wyckoff **spring** / SMC **liquidity grab**). Rejection candle closes back inside range.
3. Displacement up leaves **FVG** — Sign of Strength. Mark bullish order block at origin of displacement = **POI #1**.
4. Pullback retraces 50–61.8% into POI; smaller FVG inside = **POI #2** (double POI from S03).
5. Elliott read (simple): first strong leg up = wave 1; this pullback = wave 2; next BOS continuation targets wave 3 extension.
6. Entry: limit at double POI or confirmation on M15 BOS bounce. Stop below spring low. TP at next liquidity pool above range high.

**What NOT to do:** Label every wiggle as five waves and chase wave C as wave 3. If structure is messy, drop Elliott and trade pure SMC structure from S02.

**Practice drill (15 min):** Open XAUUSD H4 replay. Find one range with clear spring or upthrust. Mark Wyckoff phase letter on chart (A–E sketch). Overlay simple wave 1–2–3 only after BOS confirms. Screenshot before POI entry. Compare: did Wyckoff + Elliott filter agree with your SMC double POI from S03? If not, which rule told you to skip? Log answer in journal — this is how Extra Knowledge becomes habit, not theory you forget after quiz.

**Jayce note from NCI Level 3 path:** Students who master Wyckoff/Elliott **simple** version before live money report fewer overtrades in chop — because they recognize accumulation **range** and stop hunting POIs in the middle. VIP library has extended Wyckoff schematics; this lesson is the minimum viable filter for Udemy Level 2 completion.

## Define MOMENTUM of the waves

### What it is?

**Momentum of the waves** means measuring how **strong, clean, and committed** each price leg is — not guessing with RSI alone. In Jayce's SMC system, wave momentum answers:

- Is this impulse leg strong enough to trust trend-follow POI?
- Is this pullback shallow and orderly (high momentum trend) or choppy and deep (weak / ranging)?
- Should I use **aggressive limit at POI** or **wait for BOS bounce confirmation**?

Momentum here is **price-action momentum**: size of candles, speed of move, imbalance left behind, respect of POI, and how fast liquidity is taken. Smart money leaves footprints — wide bodies, small wicks in direction of trend, FVGs that do not get fully filled before continuation.

**High momentum wave signs:**

- BOS with **displacement** (large candle, closes near extreme).
- Pullback is **shallow** (38–50% max) and holds inside premium/discount correctly.
- Imbalance from impulse leg is **not** fully mitigated before next leg.
- POI reacts with **immediate rejection** — one or two candles, not 10 candles of noise.

**Low momentum wave signs:**

- Multiple CHoCH whipsaws on same TF.
- Full retrace into origin of move (100% mitigation).
- POI zone is **wide and slow** — price floats inside OB for many candles.
- Equal highs/lows everywhere — no clear liquidity sweep narrative.

Jayce connects momentum to **over-zone** concept from course quizzes: when momentum dies at HTF supply/demand, do not keep buying POIs in the middle of nowhere.

### How to define or apply it?

**Momentum scorecard (use before every setup)**

Rate each leg 1–5 on your chart screenshot:

| Factor | 1 (weak) | 5 (strong) |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Displacement at BOS | Small body, long wicks | Large body, close at extreme |
| FVG quality | Filled quickly | Open, unfilled or partial |
| Pullback depth | >61.8% or messy | 38–50%, clean swing |
| POI reaction | Slow grind | Sharp rejection + mini BOS |
| HTF alignment | Against D1 bias | With D1/H4 bias |

**Total 20–25:** Strong momentum — eligible for **set & forget limit** at double POI (see S07).  
**Total 12–19:** Medium — require **M15 BOS bounce** confirmation.  
**Below 12:** Low momentum — **no trade** or scalp only with tight rules.

**Multi-timeframe momentum rule**

1. Define momentum on **HTF first** (H4/D1 impulse leg).
2. Drop to execution TF (M15/M5) only to refine entry — never let LTF weak chop override HTF strong trend without CHoCH.
3. If HTF momentum is strong but LTF is weak inside POI, **wait** for LTF BOS in your direction — this is the "patience tax" that saves you from early stop-outs.

**Momentum + market cycle (S04)**

From market cycle theory: after manipulation and POI entry, institutions and retail trade **together** in direction of trend — that phase has **highest momentum**. Late in trend, momentum fades — wicks grow, FVGs fill, POI fails. Jayce reduces size or stops trading that pair when momentum score drops under threshold for the week.

### Example

**US30 M15 — high momentum continuation vs low momentum trap**

**Case A — High momentum (take trade):**

- H4 BOS bullish with gap-style displacement leaving FVG.
- M15 pulls back 45% into bullish OB; only 3 candles inside zone.
- Mini BOS up from POI with imbalance. Momentum score: 22/25.
- Action: sell-side liquidity above recent high is target; entry on POI or first retest with stop below OB.

**Case B — Low momentum (skip):**

- Same HTF bias bullish, but M15 shows overlapping candles, full fill of FVG, POI tested 8 times.
- CHoCH up then CHoCH down inside same zone. Score: 9/25.
- Action: **stand down**. Jayce marks "weak momentum — wait for new liquidity sweep + fresh POI."

**Journal field:** Every backtest trade, log momentum score. After 30 trades you will see your win rate clusters above 18/25 — that is your personal cutoff.

**Over-zone connection (quiz topic):** When HTF momentum dies at supply (gold at D1 high, US30 at ATH approach), POIs below look tempting but lack **wave power** — price grinds, wicks both ways, FVGs fill. Jayce calls this **over-zone** — you are late in the impulse. Momentum score caps at 12 even if OB looks pretty. Correct action: stand aside or switch to scalping only with tighter confirmation until new liquidity sweep resets narrative.

**Indicator warning:** RSI or MACD divergence alone is **not** momentum in this course. You may use RSI as secondary confluence only when price structure already scored ≥15. Many losing USDCHF trades (S06) had "oversold RSI" but momentum score under 12 — structure beats oscillator every time in Jayce's backtest samples.

## Mistake and Successful system

### What it is?

This lesson separates **retail failure patterns** from a **successful SMC trading system** Jayce uses with NCI students and prop firm challenges. A mistake is not one losing trade — it is a **repeatable process error**. A successful system is not a holy-grail indicator — it is **fixed rules** for definitions, entry type, risk, session, and review that survive losing streaks.

**Top mistakes SMC traders make (Jayce's list from course + live sessions):**

1. **Trading POI without HTF bias** — marking every order block on M5 and calling it smart money.
2. **Ignoring liquidity narrative** — entering OB with no sweep or manipulation story.
3. **Chasing after missed entry** — market orders after price already left POI (revenge FOMO).
4. **No invalidation** — moving stop when price "should" come back.
5. **Over-leverage on "strong signal"** — confusing signal power with account risk %.
6. **Mixing strategies** — trend-follow POI on a break-trend day because Telegram said so.
7. **Skipping backtest** — live money before 1–3 months demo journal proof.
8. **No journal** — cannot find root cause on USDCHF loss (see S06).
9. **Trading every pair** — no focus list (XAUUSD, US30, major forex only).
10. **Set & forget on weak POI** — passive limit in low momentum chop (S07 fixes this).

**Successful system components:**

| Pillar | What fixed looks like |
|--------|------------------------|
| Analysis | S02 definitions marked daily on HTF |
| Setup | Only double POI + BOS bounce OR break-trend rules from S03–S04 |
| Filter | Momentum score + Wyckoff/Elliott context from S05 |
| Execution | Confirmation vs set & forget per S07 checklist |
| Risk | Fixed % per trade (course lot calculator S19); max daily loss |
| Psychology | Discord competition discipline — process over PnL screenshot |
| Review | S12 journal: every trade scored, loss root cause same day |

Jayce's phrase: **"System creates change, mindset is key"** — the system is written before the session; mindset follows when you trust the skip rules.

### How to define or apply it?

**Build your one-page system (do this before S06 examples)**

1. **Pairs:** max 3 (e.g., XAUUSD, US30, EURCAD).
2. **Sessions:** London + NY overlap only for scalping; no Asian chop unless swing.
3. **Setups allowed:** (a) Trend-follow double POI, (b) BOS bounce, (c) Break trend with CHoCH — pick max 2 for first 90 days.
4. **Entry rule:** Default = wait BOS bounce until momentum score ≥ 18; set & forget only when ≥ 22 + institutional base (S07).
5. **Risk:** 1–2% per trade; stop always beyond POI + liquidity; no add to loser.
6. **Daily stop:** −3R or −4% account — walk away (Sep 26 live: first trade loss 1.5% — system allowed stop, next trades recovered with rules).
7. **Weekly:** 30 min review — count mistakes by category, one fix for next week only.

**Mistake → fix mapping (use in journal)**

| Mistake tag | Corrective action |
|-------------|-------------------|
| NO_HTF_BIAS | Mark D1/H4 before M15 entry |
| NO_LIQUIDITY | Require sweep or equal highs/lows taken |
| CHASE_ENTRY | Missed = wait re-entry rules (US30 S06) |
| MOVED_STOP | Next 10 trades: hard auto SL, no manual move |
| WEAK_POI | Apply S07 power checklist; skip if < threshold |
| OVERTRADE | Max 3 trades per session |

**Success metric for apprentices:** Not "profit this week" — **% of trades that followed full checklist**. Jayce targets **90%+ process adherence** on demo before funded account. Profit is a lagging indicator; process is leading.

### Example

**Two traders, same XAUUSD double POI chart after S03 lesson**

**Trader A (mistake system):**

- Sees bullish OB on M5, buys immediately.
- No liquidity sweep marked; D1 bearish.
- Stop inside POI; moves stop when −0.5%.
- Loss. Blames "manipulation." Re-enters short without plan. Second loss.
- Quits SMC, buys another course.

**Trader B (successful system):**

- Marks H4: liquidity sweep below, BOS up, double POI in discount.
- Momentum score 21; waits M15 BOS bounce.
- Risk 1.5%, stop below sweep low, TP at H4 liquidity.
- Trade wins or loses — journal tags: HTF yes, double POI yes, confirmation yes.
- After 50 trades, reviews tags; cuts only "momentum < 15" bucket — equity curve stabilizes.

**Jayce's competition insight:** Under CTI-style pressure, Trader B survives because **rules are binary** — pass/fail before click. Trader A trades feelings. Your homework: run Trader B protocol on demo **minimum 3 months** (course requirement from S01).

| Day | Task |
|-----|------|

| Mon–Fri | Pre-session checklist; max 3 trades; journal same day |

**Prop firm angle:** FTMO-style rules punish **daily drawdown** more than win rate. Jayce's system prioritizes **skip rules** and fixed % risk so one USDCHF-style mistake cannot cascade into account fail. Double POI with optimized RR is useless if you take five weak POIs per day — successful system is **frequency control** plus quality filter from S07.
