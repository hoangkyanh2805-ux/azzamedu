## Liquidity - smart money

### What it is?

**Liquidity** is where resting orders live on the chart — stop losses above swing highs, below swing lows, and around equal highs/lows that retail traders treat as "support" and "resistance." Smart money does not trade against random noise; they trade **where the crowd's orders are stacked**. When price sweeps those pools, institutions can fill large positions at favorable prices. That sweep is not bad luck for the market — it is **by design**. Retail traders place stops at obvious levels because textbooks taught them to; institutions know exactly where those clusters sit and engineer price to collect them before the real directional move begins.

In Smart Money Concepts, liquidity is Phase 0 of the footprint: **test liquidity**. Price runs stops, collects orders, and reveals who was wrong. You are not predicting every wick; you are marking where smart money **might** hunt before the real move. Retail sees a breakout; smart money sees a liquidity grab that sets up imbalance and manipulation on the next leg. Every professional SMC operator starts the trading day by asking one question: where is the liquidity, and which side has not been swept yet? Without that map, every candle looks like opportunity. With it, you wait for the hunt to complete before risking capital.

Liquidity pairs with every other definition in this section. Without liquidity marks, your orderblock is random. With them, you know **why** price spiked through a level before reversing — the stop hunt completed, and now absorbing or POI entry becomes possible. On XAUUSD especially, Asian session range highs and lows are liquidity magnets for London and New York. Gold does not move because of "news sentiment" alone — it moves because smart money needed to fill size at a price where retail stops provided the counterparty. Jayce teaches liquidity first because it is the foundation of **signal power**: a POI without a prior liquidity sweep is a lower-conviction setup than one where BSL or SSL was clearly taken.

The relationship between liquidity and profit % is direct. Traders who enter before the sweep get stopped out when smart money hunts. Traders who enter after the sweep, at POI, with structure confirmed — those are the Set & Forget entries that compound. Liquidity is not a reason to trade; it is a reason to **wait**. Mark the pool, watch the sweep, then let the footprint unfold through absorbing, imbalance, manipulation, and finally POI where retail and institutions trade together in the same direction. That sequence is the Jayce Pham system in miniature, and it starts here with liquidity.

### How to define or apply it?

1. **Mark buy-side liquidity (BSL)** above swing highs, equal highs, and obvious resistance where buy-stops cluster.
2. **Mark sell-side liquidity (SSL)** below swing lows, equal lows, and obvious support where sell-stops cluster.
3. On higher timeframe (H4/D1), identify the **main liquidity target** smart money is likely drawing price toward before reversal.
4. Wait for **sweep + rejection** — wick through the level, close back inside range, or immediate displacement away.
5. Combine with session timing (London/NY) for XAUUSD — gold often sweeps Asian range highs/lows first.
6. Label liquidity pools on your chart with horizontal lines and note the date they were formed — stale pools lose relevance after BOS resets structure.
7. Distinguish **internal liquidity** (within current range) from **external liquidity** (beyond range extremes) — external sweeps often trigger larger moves.
8. After sweep, watch for **displacement** — the move away from the liquidity pool should be impulsive, leaving imbalance behind.
9. Never confuse a wick with a sweep — a true sweep takes stops and shows rejection; a wick without volume at session open may be noise.
10. Pair liquidity marks with **premium/discount** — BSL sweeps at premium in uptrend often signal distribution, not continuation.
11. Journal every liquidity mark: date, level, swept (Y/N), what happened next (imbalance? manipulation?).
12. On forex pairs, mark liquidity at round numbers and prior day/week highs and lows — institutions target these deliberately.
13. **Chart rule:** Never enter *on* the liquidity line; enter after sweep confirms and structure shifts (CHoCH) toward your POI.
14. **Chart rule:** If price respects liquidity without sweep, trend may continue — do not force reversal narrative.
15. **Chart rule:** Multiple equal highs/lows = stronger BSL/SSL — smart money will almost always sweep before major reversal.

### Example

**Historical XAUUSD — H4/H1/M15, London open, March 2024.**

Gold had built equal highs at **2348.50** over three H4 sessions — classic BSL where buy-stops clustered above the obvious resistance. Retail longs from 2340 placed protective stops just below **2345.00** (SSL under the range low). On D1, gold was in late markup approaching premium of the dealing range 2300–2350. Jayce's read: smart money would sweep BSL before any meaningful reversal — the crowd was too comfortable long.

**Phase 0 — Test liquidity:** At 08:00 GMT London open, H1 spiked to **2349.20**, wicked cleanly above equal highs at 2348.50, then **closed back below 2348.00** within the same hour. Buy-stops triggered, late longs trapped, market orders filled institutional short interest. M15 showed immediate bearish engulfing off the sweep high — first sign smart money was done collecting BSL.

**Phase 1–2 — Absorbing + Imbalance:** Within two H1 candles, bearish displacement left an **imbalance (FVG)** between **2347.80–2346.90**. Price did not collapse straight down — it absorbed at the FVG CE (2347.35) for 90 minutes, allowing institutions to distribute remaining long inventory into retail buy orders still hoping for "another leg up."

**Phase 3 — Manipulation:** A sharp M15 rally pushed price back to **2348.00** (premium), failed to reclaim the swept high, and printed a lower high — classic manipulation into premium before expansion down. Retail interpreted this as "bull flag"; SMC read it as last trap before markdown.

**Phase 4–5 — POI entry:** M15 **bearish CHoCH** broke the higher low at **2346.50**. H1 bearish orderblock formed at **2347.20–2347.80** (last up-close candle before displacement) — this became POI in premium. Entry short at **2347.50**, stop above sweep high **2349.50**, target SSL pool at **2340.00** then runner at **2335.00**.

**Result:** Price expanded to 2340 within 6 hours. RR **1:3.75** to TP1, **1:6.25** to TP2. Set & Forget — no stop adjustment. Signal power **6/6**: liquidity sweep ✓, FVG ✓, manipulation ✓, premium POI ✓, CHoCH ✓, HTF alignment ✓.

Traders who shorted the sweep at 2349 got stopped on the manipulation wick. Traders who waited for CHoCH + POI at 2347.50 captured the leg. That is the liquidity lesson: the sweep is not the entry — it is the **permission** to start looking for POI.

## Imbalance (IMB) - Fair Value Gap (FVG)

### What it is?

**Imbalance** (IMB), also called **Fair Value Gap (FVG)**, is a three-candle pattern where price moved so fast that it left an **unfilled zone** — a gap between the wick of candle 1 and the wick of candle 3, with candle 2 as the impulse. The market often **returns to rebalance** that inefficiency before continuing. Smart money leaves these gaps on purpose: aggressive entries during news or session open create imbalance; later, price retraces to **mitigate** the zone while institutions add or defend positions. The gap is "unfair" because one side of the market never got to transact at those prices — rebalancing is the market's way of offering that opportunity before choosing direction.

Imbalance sits in Phase 2 of the SMC footprint — after liquidity test and often during **absorbing** or early displacement. Not every FVG gets filled 100%; the **valid** ones align with HTF trend, premium/discount, and a POI (orderblock) at the edge of the gap. Retail traders see "support"; you see **unfinished business** that smart money may use for optimal entry price. When Jayce marks an FVG on XAUUSD during the London open, he is not predicting a magical support line — he is noting where institutions left orders unfilled and where price is likely to return before the next expansion leg.

FVG is also a **filter** for signal power: if your POI sits inside a fresh bullish FVG in discount during uptrend, signal power is high. If FVG opposes HTF bias, treat it as trap — price may poke in and continue against you. The **consequent encroachment (CE)** at 50% of the FVG is Jayce's preferred entry refinement inside the gap — not the edge, not the full fill, but the midpoint where institutions often defend before continuation. Partial mitigation at CE plus rejection from an orderblock produces higher win rate than blind limit orders at the FVG boundary.

Understanding imbalance transforms how you read pullbacks. Retail sees "price pulling back" and panics. SMC sees price mitigating an inefficiency before smart money resumes the move. The FVG is the bridge between displacement (Phase 2) and POI entry (Phase 4–5). Double order-block setups in S03 often place POI **inside** the FVG overlap — the gap provides the magnet, the orderblock provides the trigger, and together they optimize RR by narrowing your entry zone while keeping the same liquidity target.

### How to define or apply it?

1. Identify **bullish FVG**: gap between high of candle 1 and low of candle 3 (middle candle bullish impulse up).
2. Identify **bearish FVG**: gap between low of candle 1 and high of candle 3 (middle candle bearish impulse down).
3. Draw the box from wick to wick — that is your IMB zone; shade it on chart for visual clarity.
4. Classify **CE (consequent encroachment)** at 50% of FVG for refined entry; full fill = 100% mitigation.
5. Only trade FVG reactions when **BOS or CHoCH** already confirms direction on your execution TF.
6. Measure FVG size relative to ATR — gaps larger than 1× ATR on H1 are institutional; tiny gaps on M1 are noise.
7. Mark whether FVG is **fresh** (untouched) or **mitigated** (partially/fully filled) — only fresh or 50% mitigated gaps retain power.
8. Stack FVG with OB: when orderblock edge aligns with FVG boundary, that overlap is premium POI real estate.
9. On XAUUSD, FVGs created during London/NY session opens have higher fill probability than Asian session gaps.
10. Delete invalidated FVGs when price closes through opposite side with displacement — the market rejected that inefficiency narrative.
11. Use FVG as **target** for partial profits when trading toward liquidity — price often pauses at unfilled gaps.
12. **Chart rule:** HTF FVG > LTF FVG for swing; scalp only LTF FVG inside HTF POI.
13. **Chart rule:** Partial fill (50%) + rejection from orderblock = stronger than blind limit at top of gap.
14. **Chart rule:** FVG against HTF trend is a trap zone — mark it but do not trade it without CHoCH confirmation.
15. **Chart rule:** Three or more stacked FVGs in same direction = strong imbalance corridor — expect reaction at CE of deepest gap.

### Example

**Historical EURUSD — D1/H4/M15 during NY session, November 2023.**

**Context:** EURUSD in H4 downtrend, series of lower highs from **1.0900** to **1.0850**. SSL rested at **1.0810** (prior week low). D1 bearish bias after ECB hawkish surprise failed to sustain euro rally — smart money distributing into retail euro longs.

**Phase 0 — Liquidity:** Asian session swept minor SSL at **1.0825** without strong rejection — internal liquidity taken, not the main pool.

**Phase 2 — Imbalance creation:** After US CPI release at 13:30 GMT, EURUSD dropped three M15 candles in 45 minutes: C1 high **1.0850**, C2 strong bearish impulse body (CPI beat expectations, dollar strength), C3 low **1.0820**. Bearish FVG formed between **1.0842–1.0835** — a clean 7-pip gap on M15, equivalent to 25+ pips on H4 when aggregated. H4 candle closed as bearish engulfing, confirming institutional participation.

**Phase 3 — Manipulation:** Price did not collapse immediately. During NY lunch (17:00–19:00 GMT), EURUSD rallied into the FVG — classic manipulation against the true bearish direction. Price tagged **50% CE at 1.0838**, wicked to **1.0840** (FVG top), and rejected hard off a **bearish orderblock** at the FVG upper boundary — same zone marked as POI.

**Phase 4 — Structure:** M15 **bearish CHoCH** had printed at **1.0832** breaking the prior higher low before price returned to FVG. This CHoCH was the gate — no CHoCH, no short. BOS at **1.0828** confirmed bearish continuation after POI rejection.

**Trade:** Entry short at POI **1.0837** (OB + FVG overlap), stop above OB high **1.0845**, target SSL at **1.0810**, runner at **1.0795**. RR **1:3.4** to TP1, **1:5.6** to TP2.

**Result:** Price expanded to 1.0810 within 4 hours, filled runner by next London open. The imbalance was the **magnet** that pulled price back for optimal institutional entry; the orderblock was the **trigger** that confirmed smart money defense. Without marking IMB first, you would have chased the CPI dump at 1.0825 (poor RR, no POI) or entered too early at 1.0845 before mitigation (stopped on manipulation wick). FVG + POI + CHoCH = Set & Forget short with maximum signal power.

## Manipulation phase

### What it is?

The **manipulation phase** is smart money's fake move — a deliberate push **against** the true direction to trap retail before the real leg. It often follows liquidity sweep and appears as a sharp spike, false breakout, or return into FVG that **fails** at premium (in downtrend) or discount (in uptrend). Wyckoff students call it spring/upthrust; SMC calls it manipulation between Phase 1 (absorbing) and Phase 3 (lack of liquidity / expansion). The manipulation candle is the most expensive candle for retail traders because it looks like "confirmation" of their bias right before the market reverses hard against them.

Manipulation is why retail loses on "perfect" breakouts: they buy the break of resistance while institutions **sell into their market orders**. Your edge is patience — wait until manipulation completes, structure shifts (CHoCH), and price offers POI in the correct premium/discount quadrant. Jayce stresses: **do not trade the manipulation candle**; trade the reaction after smart money shows their hand. On XAUUSD during New York session, the manipulation phase often lasts 15–45 minutes on M5 — long enough to trap impatient scalpers, short enough that Set & Forget swing traders who waited for POI capture the entire expansion leg.

This phase connects directly to **signal power**. A setup with sweep + FVG + manipulation + POI + BOS has stacked confluence. A setup with only one element is retail gambling. Manipulation is also the **last test** before POI entry: if price manipulates into your zone and rejects, institutions have shown their commitment. If price manipulates through your zone with body closes, your POI is invalid and you must re-mark structure. The manipulation high (in bearish setups) or manipulation low (in bullish setups) becomes your **stop reference** — place stops beyond that wick, not just beyond the orderblock.

Jayce teaches manipulation as the emotional filter of the system. When you feel FOMO watching price spike without you, that spike is often manipulation. When you feel fear watching price return to your POI after a sweep, that return is often the entry. Retail and institutions trade together **after** manipulation completes — not during it. The manipulation phase separates gamblers from operators. Operators mark it, wait through it, and execute at POI. Gamblers chase it and fund the operators' positions.

### How to define or apply it?

1. After liquidity sweep, watch for **counter-trend spike** into premium/discount or into FVG.
2. Label manipulation when price **fails** to hold beyond key level — long wick, engulfing rejection, or immediate CHoCH.
3. Mark the **manipulation high/low** — your stop reference for POI entry on the other side.
4. Confirm with **volume/session** — London fake-out of Asian high is classic XAUUSD manipulation.
5. Pair with **orderblock** at manipulation extreme — that OB often becomes POI for Set & Forget entry.
6. Distinguish manipulation from **genuine continuation** — continuation shows body closes beyond level + FVG; manipulation shows wick + close back inside.
7. Time manipulation phases — on M5/M15 they typically last 3–12 candles; longer may indicate range, not trap.
8. Watch for **equal highs/lows** forming during manipulation — these become the next liquidity targets after CHoCH.
9. Manipulation into FVG CE is higher probability than manipulation to random S/R — the gap attracts price magnetically.
10. After manipulation completes, require **CHoCH on execution TF** before placing orders — manipulation alone is not entry.
11. Record manipulation in journal as "M-phase" with screenshot — builds pattern recognition for live sessions.
12. **Chart rule:** Manipulation without displacement after = range, not trend — stand aside.
13. **Chart rule:** Two manipulations in same direction may mean accumulation; wait for BOS before entry.
14. **Chart rule:** Manipulation that creates new FVG in true direction = high signal power — institutions left imbalance during the trap.
15. **Chart rule:** Never add to losing position during manipulation spike — wait for POI or accept stop.

### Example

**Historical XAUUSD — H4/H1/M5, NY reversal, September 2024.**

**Context:** Gold in H4 downtrend from **2360** high. SSL pool at **2328.00** (prior week low, equal lows on H1). Retail shorts placed stops above **2335.00**. D1 approaching discount of larger dealing range — smart money accumulating short interest before next markdown leg.

**Phase 0 — Test liquidity:** Overnight Asian/London handoff swept SSL at **2328.00**, wicked to **2327.40**, closed back above **2328.50** on H1 — sell-stops collected, late shorts trapped. Liquidity sweep confirmed on H4 wick.

**Phase 1 — Absorbing:** Price consolidated **2328–2331** for 4 hours on H1. No displacement yet — institutions absorbing remaining short inventory at discounted prices after the sweep.

**Phase 3 — Manipulation:** At 14:30 GMT (US session open), gold **rallied aggressively** from 2330 to **2334.20** in 25 minutes on M5 — a vertical spike that broke above Asian session high and triggered retail breakout buys. This was manipulation **up** into H1 **premium** (above 50% EQ of the 2325–2335 dealing range). Retail chased longs screaming "breakout, gold is back." Smart money sold into every market buy order.

**Rejection:** At **2334.00**, H1 bearish orderblock rejected the spike — last up-close candle before the sweep down. M5 printed shooting star, then bearish engulfing. Price failed to hold above **2333.50** (manipulation high). M5 **bearish CHoCH** broke higher low at **2332.80** — permission to look for short POI.

**Phase 2 — Imbalance:** Bearish FVG formed on M5 between **2333.00–2332.20** during the rejection candle sequence.

**Phase 4–5 — POI entry:** POI at **2333.50** (bearish OB + FVG overlap in premium). Entry short **2333.50**, stop above manipulation high **2334.80**, TP1 internal SSL **2325.00**, TP2 major SSL **2320.00**.

**Result:** Gold expanded to 2325 within NY session, hit 2320 by next London open. RR **1:6.5** to TP1, **1:10+** to TP2. Set & Forget.

Manipulation was the **last trap** before expansion down. Traders who entered long on the spike at 2334 paid smart money. Traders who waited for CHoCH + POI at 2333.50 captured the full leg. ## BOS and CHoCH

### What it is?

**BOS (Break of Structure)** confirms **continuation** of the current trend — price breaks the most recent swing high in uptrend (bullish BOS) or swing low in downtrend (bearish BOS). Smart money uses BOS to show trend is intact and to hunt liquidity on the **next** pullback POI. When you see bullish BOS on H4, institutions are signaling they want higher prices and are willing to defend discount POIs on pullbacks. BOS is not an entry signal — it is a **bias confirmation** that tells you which direction to look for POI.

**CHoCH (Change of Character)** signals **potential reversal** — the first break **against** the prevailing trend structure. In downtrend, bullish CHoCH breaks the last lower high; in uptrend, bearish CHoCH breaks the last higher low. CHoCH after manipulation + liquidity sweep is your **permission** to look for counter-trend or new-trend entries at POI. One CHoCH does not guarantee full trend reversal — it guarantees that **character changed** on that timeframe and you must respect the new structure until proven otherwise.

BOS and CHoCH are not labels for Instagram charts — they are **gates**. No CHoCH after sweep = no reversal trade. No BOS in trend direction = no continuation add. Double order-block strategy in S03–S04 depends entirely on reading which one printed last on your execution timeframe while HTF still governs bias. Jayce's rule: HTF CHoCH + LTF POI = high signal power for reversal. HTF BOS + LTF pullback POI = high signal power for continuation. LTF CHoCH against HTF BOS = caution — either scalp only or wait for HTF to align.

The body close requirement separates real structure breaks from liquidity wicks. A wick above swing high that closes back below is a **liquidity grab**, not BOS. A candle that **closes** above the swing high with displacement is BOS — institutions committed to continuation. Same logic for CHoCH: wick below higher low = potential manipulation; body close below = character changed. This single distinction saves more accounts than any indicator. Profit % improves when you only act on body-close BOS/CHoCH and ignore wick-only breaks that trap retail.

### How to define or apply it?

1. Map **swing highs and lows** on execution TF (M15/H1) using obvious pivots — not every minor wick.
2. **Bullish BOS:** close above prior swing high in established uptrend.
3. **Bearish BOS:** close below prior swing low in established downtrend.
4. **Bullish CHoCH:** in downtrend, close above last significant lower high.
5. **Bearish CHoCH:** in uptrend, close below last significant higher low.
6. Require **body close** beyond level — wick-only breaks are weak (liquidity grab, not structure).
7. After BOS, expect **pullback to POI** — do not chase; wait for discount/premium alignment.
8. After CHoCH, expect **return to POI** in premium (shorts) or discount (longs) before expansion.
9. Label BOS/CHoCH with date and price on chart — revisit to see which ones held and which were false.
10. HTF BOS/CHoCH overrides LTF — always check H4/D1 before acting on M15 structure.
11. Sequential CHoCH on multiple TFs (M15 + H1 + H4) = trend reversal conviction; single LTF CHoCH = correction only.
12. BOS without FVG/displacement = weak — may be false; wait for POI confirmation.
13. **Chart rule:** HTF CHoCH + LTF POI = high signal power; LTF CHoCH against HTF BOS = caution or scalp only.
14. **Chart rule:** BOS at premium in uptrend = late chase risk; prefer pullback POI entry after BOS confirms.
15. **Chart rule:** CHoCH without prior liquidity sweep = lower conviction reversal — reduce size or skip.

### Example

**Panel A — BOS continuation (follow trend):**

D1 GBPUSD in clear uptrend: HH at **1.2680**, HL at **1.2580**, HH at **1.2720**. H4 bullish structure intact. H1 **bullish BOS** printed when price closed above prior swing high at **1.2720** with displacement — FVG left between **1.2705–1.2712**. This BOS confirmed markup phase continuation; Jayce's bias: look for long POI on pullback only.

Pullback over 18 hours brought price into **discount** of the 1.2680–1.2720 leg. H1 **bullish orderblock** formed at **1.2685–1.2690** (last down-close candle before BOS impulse). H4 nested bullish OB at **1.2680–1.2688** — **double OB POI** in discount. M15 printed minor bullish CHoCH (continuation character, not reversal) at **1.2688**, then BOS above **1.2695**.

Confusing the two is how traders hold longs through reversals or short into continuations. BOS = stay with trend, find POI. CHoCH = respect new character, find reversal POI. Signal power maximum when both panels' rules followed sequentially, not simultaneously.

## Pullback and Break out

### What it is?

**Pullback** is price retracing **with** the institutional move — a temporary move against trend that returns to POI, FVG, or orderblock before continuation. Smart money pulls back to **discount in uptrend** (buy cheaper) or **premium in downtrend** (sell richer). Pullback trading is the core of **follow trend** strategy: after BOS, you wait for pullback, not chase breakout. The pullback is not weakness — it is institutions repricing before the next expansion. Jayce's highest win-rate trades are all pullbacks to double OB POI after confirmed BOS.

**Breakout** is price leaving a defined range or key level with displacement — often what retail trades. In SMC, many "breakouts" are **manipulation** or liquidity events. The skilled trader asks: is this breakout **BOS with displacement and FVG**, or a fake break to sweep liquidity? Real breakout follows absorbing, leaves imbalance, and pulls back shallowly before expansion. Fake breakout sweeps and reverses — CHoCH follows. The breakout candle is the most seductive candle on the chart because it looks like "the move is happening now" — but without footprint context, it is usually a trap.

Jayce separates **trading follow trend** (pullback to POI) from **trading break the trend** (CHoCH + reversal POI). Pullback = higher win rate, optimized RR; breakout chase = lower win rate unless confirmed by full SMC footprint. On XAUUSD, retail breakout traders buy above Asian range high every London open; SMC operators wait for sweep, manipulation, CHoCH, then POI entry — often 15–30 points better price with 3× the RR.

The interplay between pullback and breakout defines your **profit %** over a month. Ten pullback entries at 1:4 RR with 70% win rate outperform twenty breakout chases at 1:1.5 RR with 45% win rate. Set & Forget works on pullbacks because your stop is tight (below OB) and your target is far (next liquidity pool). Breakout entries require wider stops (beyond the break level) and offer less room to target. The course builds S03 and S04 entirely on pullback mechanics — breakout is what you avoid unless the full phase sequence confirms it as genuine BOS, not manipulation.

### How to define or apply it?

1. **Pullback entry:** After BOS, mark POI in discount (long) or premium (short); enter on mitigation, stop beyond OB.
2. **Breakout validation:** Require close beyond level + FVG + no immediate CHoCH back inside range.
3. Measure pullback depth — **50% of impulse**, FVG CE, or OB edge; avoid random Fib without structure.
4. **Failed breakout:** Wick beyond level, close inside = manipulation; plan reversal at opposite POI.
5. Use **multiple timeframe:** HTF BOS, LTF pullback POI for entry timing.
6. Shallow pullback (38–50% retrace) in strong trend = sign of strength; expect quick BOS bounce from POI.
7. Deep pullback (62–79% retrace) = test of HTF POI; wider stop but larger TP potential.
8. Pullback into FVG CE inside OB = highest confluence pullback entry in the system.
9. Count candles in pullback — healthy pullback on H1 is 5–15 candles; >20 may signal CHoCH coming.
10. **Breakout chase filter:** If you missed pullback POI, do not chase — wait for next BOS + pullback cycle.
11. Set & Forget targets at next liquidity pool, not arbitrary pip counts.
12. **Chart rule:** Shallow pullback in strong trend = sign of strength; deep pullback to HTF POI = stronger reaction expected.
13. **Chart rule:** Do not buy breakout at premium in uptrend — you are late; wait pullback to discount.
14. **Chart rule:** Breakout without prior liquidity sweep in range = likely fake — wait for sweep first.
15. **Chart rule:** Pullback that breaks POI with body close = OB invalid — re-mark after new BOS, do not hope-trade.

### Example

**Historical XAUUSD — H4/H1/M15, follow trend pullback vs breakout trap, August 2024.**

**Panel A — Follow trend pullback (winner):**

H4 uptrend established: BOS at **2360**, prior SSL swept at **2345**. Dealing range **2345–2360**, EQ at **2352.50**. H4 bullish BOS confirmed markup continuation. Price pulled back 38% to **bullish double OB** at **2352–2354** (discount zone — below EQ). H1 nested OB **2352.50–2353.50**, bullish FVG overlap **2352.00–2352.80**. No CHoCH on H1 during pullback — structure intact, character still bullish.

M15 BOS above **2355.00** after POI touch — BOS bounce trigger (S04 preview). Entry long at **2353.00**, stop **2349.00** (below H1 OB), target BSL **2372.00** (equal highs). RR **1:4.75**. Result: +19 points, TP hit in 36 hours. Set & Forget.

## Premium and Discount

### What it is?

**Premium and Discount** divide the current dealing range — usually from last significant swing low to swing high (or vice versa) — into **expensive** and **cheap** zones relative to equilibrium (50%). Smart money **buys in discount** (below 50%) and **sells in premium** (above 50%) within that range. Retail does the opposite: buys breakout at premium, sells panic at discount. This single inversion of behavior explains most retail account destruction. Institutions have infinite patience to wait for discount; retail has infinite impatience to chase premium.

Equilibrium (EQ) at 50% is not magic — it is the **fair value** midpoint of the leg smart money is trading. Fibonacci 0.5 overlaps EQ. In uptrend pullback, you want POI in **discount** (0–50% of range from low to high). In downtrend rally, you want POI in **premium** (50–100%). Trading premium longs in a bearish HTF leg is fighting institutions. Jayce calls premium/discount the "cheapest filter" in the system — it costs nothing to draw but saves the most money.

Premium/discount is the **filter** that turns orderblocks from random rectangles into high-probability POIs. Double order-block with POI (S03) explicitly optimizes RR by entering only when price is on the **correct side of EQ** for your direction. An OB at the right price but wrong half of the range is a **trap OB** — price may touch it, react briefly, then continue against you because institutions are not defending that zone for your direction. The OB looks perfect on screenshot; the EQ line tells you it is wrong.

Advanced refinement: **0.62–0.79 discount** (or premium for shorts) captures deep pullback entries in strong trends. Not every trade needs this depth — shallow 38–50% pullbacks work in aggressive markup. But when HTF trend is mature and LTF shows CHoCH warning signs, waiting for 62–79% premium/discount POI improves signal power and RR dramatically. On XAUUSD weekly swings, the difference between entering short at 50% EQ vs 70% premium can be 30+ points of additional RR to the same SSL target. Set & Forget rewards patience at the correct EQ quadrant.

### How to define or apply it?

1. Identify **dealing range:** most recent clear swing low to swing high (for bearish context) or high to low (bullish).
2. Draw **0.5 EQ line** — above = premium, below = discount (for range from low to high).
3. **Long bias:** seek POI in discount; take profit toward premium or BSL above range.
4. **Short bias:** seek POI in premium; take profit toward discount or SSL below range.
5. Refine with **0.62–0.79 discount** for deep pullback entries in strong trends (optional confluence).
6. Reset dealing range after every **BOS** — old EQ is invalid when new swing forms.
7. Shade premium zone red, discount zone green on chart — visual discipline prevents wrong-side entries.
8. POI must sit entirely within correct half — if OB straddles EQ, wait for price to reach the correct portion.
9. Combine with FVG — best POIs sit where OB + FVG overlap in correct premium/discount.
10. On indices (US30), premium/discount of daily range is especially reliable during NY session.
11. Journal EQ price with every trade — review whether wrong-side entries correlate with losses.
12. **Chart rule:** POI in wrong half of range = skip trade regardless of how "perfect" OB looks.
13. **Chart rule:** After range break (BOS), reset dealing range to new swing — old EQ invalid.
14. **Chart rule:** Premium long only valid if HTF bullish CHoCH confirmed — otherwise it is counter-trend gamble.
15. **Chart rule:** EQ is not a entry level — it is a filter. Enter at OB inside discount/premium, not at EQ itself.

**Context:** Dow in H4 downtrend after distribution at all-time highs. Dealing range from swing low **38500** to swing high **39200** (prior week). EQ = **38850**. D1 bearish bias, H4 bearish CHoCH confirmed after BSL sweep at **39200**.

## Orderblock / POI

### What it is?

An **Orderblock (OB)** is the last opposing candle before a strong impulsive move — the zone where institutions **placed orders** before displacement. **Bullish OB:** last down-close candle before bullish impulse. **Bearish OB:** last up-close candle before bearish impulse. **POI (Point of Interest)** is your **tradable** OB — refined with liquidity, FVG, premium/discount, and BOS/CHoCH context. Not every OB is POI; POI is OB that passes all filters. The market prints hundreds of OBs per month on XAUUSD; you trade perhaps five that qualify as POI. That selectivity is the entire edge.

Orderblocks are Phase 4–5 of the footprint: after test liquidity, absorbing, imbalance, manipulation — price returns to **POI** where retail and institutions **trade together** (retail finally right direction if they waited). Smart money defends OB; price reacts with continuation or reversal depending on CHoCH vs BOS. When price mitigates POI and displaces, you see the institutional footprint complete. When price blows through POI with body closes, the OB failed — institutions are not defending that zone.

**Double order-block** (next sections) stacks two OBs — e.g., H1 POI inside H4 POI — for **optimized RR** and higher signal power. Single OB is beginner; double OB with POI is Jayce's **main strategy** for prop firm low drawdown and Set & Forget swings. The outer OB (HTF) defines where institutions have macro interest; the inner OB (LTF) defines where you place the tight stop. Same target at far liquidity, half the risk — that is optimized RR.

POI discipline separates Jayce students from indicator traders. An indicator says "buy" randomly; POI says "buy here, stop here, target there" with structural logic. **Breaker block** is advanced POI — a failed OB that flips role after CHoCH. The bullish OB that could not hold becomes bearish POI on retest. Case 1 break-trend reversals in S03 rely heavily on breaker blocks. Stale OBs mitigated three or more times lose power — always mark fresh OB after latest BOS. The newest displacement creates the newest POI. Old rectangles on your chart are archaeology, not trade plans.

### How to define or apply it?

1. Find **displacement** — strong move with FVG; mark last opposite candle before it = OB.
2. Draw zone from OB open to high (bearish) or low to open (bullish) — include wick if it held liquidity.
3. **Mitigation:** first return to OB = primary entry; full mitigation = entire zone tagged.
4. Promote OB to **POI** only if: correct premium/discount, HTF alignment, liquidity taken, CHoCH/BOS confirmed.
5. **Breaker block:** failed OB that flips role after CHoCH — advanced POI for re-entry.
6. Score POI confluence: liquidity sweep (+1), FVG overlap (+1), premium/discount (+1), double OB (+1), BOS/CHoCH (+1).
7. Minimum score 4/5 for Set & Forget; 3/5 for half size; below 3 = skip.
8. Stop beyond OB wick + buffer (2–5 pips forex, $2–5 gold) — not arbitrary pip distance.
9. Target next liquidity pool or opposing POI — not random R-multiple without structure.
10. Mark mitigation count on each OB — first touch strongest, third touch weakest.
11. HTF OB draws first, LTF OB inside second — overlap zone is the POI sweet spot.
12. **Chart rule:** Stale OB (mitigated 3+ times) loses power — mark fresh OB after latest BOS.
13. **Chart rule:** POI without liquidity sweep beforehand = lower conviction on reversals.
14. **Chart rule:** OB against HTF trend requires CHoCH on HTF — not just LTF POI.
15. **Chart rule:** Never enter at OB edge without LTF BOS bounce confirmation (S04) — default patience.
