### Objective

## M1L2 — Why systems beat alert chasing

### Objective
Understand why documented systems outperform reactive alert-chasing — as an education framework, not a performance promise.

### Video
`[PENDING: YouTube unlisted URL]` · Title: `AE Apprentice M1L2`

### Key points

- Alert chasing optimizes for **speed**; operating systems optimize for **repeatability**.
- A system includes: risk cap (2% framework), pre-trade gate, session SOP, weekly review — not just entries.
- Structured trade ideas inside SOPs are **educational examples** — you own every execution decision.
- Community (including VIP desk) provides **accountability within rules** — not a feed of guaranteed winners.
- Red flag phrases to avoid in any education you consume: "guaranteed," "passive income bot," "can't lose."
- Action: list three behaviors from your last week that were alert-chasing vs system-based.
