## M2L1 — 2% risk management framework

### Objective
Learn the definitions and educational application of the 2% risk cap — not as a profit formula.

### Video
`[PENDING: YouTube unlisted URL]` · Title: `AE Apprentice M2L1`

### Key points

- **2% rule (education):** risk no more than 2% of account equity on a single trade idea — adjust to your jurisdiction and comfort with professional advice if needed.
- Purpose: survive variance so you can execute your system long enough to learn.
- Risk = distance to invalidation × position size — not "how much I hope to make."
- The framework applies to demo, paper, and live — build the habit before size.
- This is **risk education**, not a promise of returns or drawdown limits in all market conditions.
- Write your current account size and 2% dollar amount in your journal (private — not shared in Telegram).

## M2L2 — Sizing worksheet walkthrough

### Objective
Practice position sizing using the downloadable worksheet with example scenarios.

### Video
`[PENDING: YouTube unlisted URL]` · Title: `AE Apprentice M2L2`

### Key points

- Open the **2% Sizing Worksheet** — fields: account equity, risk %, stop distance, instrument tick value.
- Walk through two **educational scenarios** (e.g. XAUUSD) with different stop distances — same risk dollars, different size.
- Never size from a screenshot or alert — size from **your** plan and invalidation level.
- If worksheet output exceeds your emotional comfort, lower risk % — consistency beats theory.
- Complete at least three practice rows before your next demo session.
- Keep worksheets private; do not post sized positions as proof in community channels.

## M2L3 — Pre-trade risk gate

### Objective
Run a one-page go/no-go checklist before any order is placed.

### Video
`[PENDING: YouTube unlisted URL]` · Title: `AE Apprentice M2L3`

### Key points

- The **Pre-Trade Risk Gate** is a hard stop: if any box fails, you do not place the trade.
- Gates include: plan written, stop defined, size calculated, within daily loss limit, not in tilt.
- "Almost setup" is not a setup — structure over frequency.
- Pair the gate with M1 desk readiness — both must pass.
- Log gate results in journal (pass/skip) — track discipline, not P&L marketing.
- Use the gate on demo for minimum 10 sessions before discussing live size with anyone.

## M2L4 — When not to trade

### Objective
Apply protocols for sitting out — structure and capital preservation over trade count.

### Video
`[PENDING: YouTube unlisted URL]` · Title: `AE Apprentice M2L4`

### Key points

- **Valid reasons to skip:** news you don't understand, no invalidation clarity, daily loss limit hit, fatigue, missing SOP step.
- Sitting out is an **operating decision**, not failure — professionals skip more than beginners think.
- After a skip, note *why* in weekly review — patterns reveal emotional triggers.
- No one scores you on trades per week; score yourself on checklist adherence.
- Avoid "revenge" sessions — if you skipped for tilt, end the day.
- Module 2 complete when: 5 consecutive demo sessions with sizing worksheet + risk gate logged.
