## M2L1 — Liquidity & participation (education)

### Objective
Understand liquidity as a **chart concept** for documenting where stops may cluster — not as a prediction of guaranteed moves.

### Lesson body

**What is liquidity (education)?**

In market structure education, *liquidity* describes price areas where many resting orders **may** exist — often around obvious swing highs/lows where stop losses cluster. We use the label to **plan and document**, not to predict that price *must* reverse.

Operators at Alpha Elite mark liquidity **before** the session opens. The question is never "will it sweep?" — it is: *If price trades here, what was my written plan?*

**How institutions may interact (illustrative only)**

Larger participants sometimes seek liquidity to fill size. Retail courses often dramatize this as "smart money hunting stops." We avoid that certainty language. Instead:

1. Mark the level on your chart.
2. Write what you would **observe** if price reaches the area.
3. Run the pre-trade risk gate — no gate pass, no trade.

**Your documentation job**

Before each XAUUSD session (demo or live):

- Mark 1–3 liquidity areas on H4 or H1.
- Note in journal: *Why this level matters structurally* (not "guaranteed bounce").
- Pair with higher-timeframe bias from your weekly review.

**Practice exercise (15 min)**

1. Open XAUUSD H4 — replay mode is fine.
2. Mark three historical liquidity sweeps.
3. For each, answer: Did I have a pre-written plan? Emotional or structured?
4. Save screenshots in your journal folder — private, not for Telegram bragging.

**Common mistakes**

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Every sweep = auto entry | Require full setup documentation + risk gate |
| Liquidity without HTF bias | Document bias first in M5 weekly review |
| Copying someone else's marked levels | Mark your own chart every session |
